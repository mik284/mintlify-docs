This guide walks you through the five most important things you will do in Lango as a new user. By the end, you will have logged in, explored the dashboard, onboarded a merchant, found a transaction, and generated a report — giving you a solid foundation to work from every day.

<Steps>

  <Step title="Log In to the Portal">
    Open your browser and navigate to your institution's Lango portal URL. You will land on the **Institution Portal** login page.

    1. Enter your **username** (all lowercase, no spaces) in the username field.
    2. Enter your **password** in the password field.
    3. Click **Submit** (or press **Enter**).
    4. Lango redirects you to the OTP verification page. Enter the **6-digit one-time password** sent to your registered email address, then click **Submit OTP**.
    5. On successful verification, Lango redirects you directly to the **Dashboard**.

    <Note>
      If this is your first login or your password has expired, Lango will redirect you to the **Change Password** page before you can proceed. Set a new password that meets the complexity requirements, then log in again.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Explore the Dashboard">
    The Dashboard is your at-a-glance view of the entire payments estate. It contains two distinct views accessible from the left sidebar under **Dashboards**:

    - **Terminals Telemetry** (`/dashboard/all-terminals`) — Shows the live status of every terminal in your estate. Select any terminal to drill down into its details, operators, and recent transactions.
    - **Transactions Dashboard** (`/dashboard/transaction-dashboard`) — Displays aggregated transaction activity, giving you a quick read on volumes, trends, and anomalies across your POS estate.

    Spend a few minutes here to familiarise yourself with the layout. Use the sidebar to move between modules at any time.

    <Tip>
      Your role determines which dashboard views you can see. If a view is not visible in the sidebar, contact your system administrator to review your workgroup permissions.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Onboard Your First Customer">
    A **customer** in Lango is a merchant or business entity that your bank has approved to accept card payments. Follow these steps to set one up completely:

    1. Click **Customer Management** in the left sidebar.
    2. Click **Create Customer** and fill in the customer's business details — name, customer type, company type, merchant category (MCC), and contact information. Submit the form.
    3. Open the newly created customer record and navigate to the **Outlets** tab. Click **Add Outlet** to register the customer's physical trading location.
    4. Within the outlet, open the **Terminals** tab and click **Assign Terminal** to link a POS device from your inventory to this outlet.

    <Note>
      If maker-checker is enabled on your system, creating a customer or assigning a terminal will generate a task that a second authorised user must approve before the record becomes active. Check the **Tasks** inbox in the sidebar to track pending approvals.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review a Transaction">
    Once terminals are active and processing, you can inspect individual transactions from the **Transactions** module.

    1. Click **Transactions** in the left sidebar to open the full transactions list.
    2. Use the search and filter controls at the top of the table to narrow results by:
       - **Reference number** — paste the exact transaction reference for an instant match.
       - **Date range** — select a start and end date to view all transactions within a period.
       - Additional filters such as terminal ID, merchant, or transaction status are also available.
    3. Click any row to open the **Transaction Details** page, where you can see the full breakdown: amount, card scheme, terminal, outlet, operator, response code, and timestamps.

    <Tip>
      You can also reach transaction details from within a customer's record, a specific outlet, or directly from a terminal on the dashboard — useful when you are already investigating a particular device or merchant.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Run Your First Report">
    Lango's **Reports** module lets you produce compliance-ready exports across customers, terminals, and transactions.

    1. Click **Reports** in the left sidebar to expand the reports menu.
    2. To review system activity, select **Audit Trails** (`/reports/audit-trail`). This log records every action taken in the portal — who did what, and when.
    3. To export transaction data, navigate to **Transactions Reports** and choose the report type that matches your need (e.g. Local Purchase, Failed Transactions, or a Summary report).
    4. Apply the relevant date range and any additional filters, then click **Export** to download the report as a CSV file.

    <Tip>
      The **Customers Reports** and **Terminals Reports** sections provide dedicated exports for merchant and device data — useful for periodic reconciliation and regulatory submissions.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

</Steps>
