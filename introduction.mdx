Lango is a web-based payment management portal built for banks and financial institutions. It gives your operations teams, compliance officers, and terminal managers a single, unified workspace to onboard merchants, deploy and monitor POS terminals, oversee transaction activity, and produce audit-ready reports — all without switching between disparate tools or spreadsheets.

## Who Lango Is For

Lango is designed for the people inside your institution who keep the payments estate running:

- **Bank Operations Teams** — onboard new merchants, manage outlet setups, and track terminal deployments end-to-end.
- **Terminal Managers** — maintain terminal inventory, apply device configurations, and monitor telemetry across your entire POS estate.
- **Compliance & Audit Officers** — generate audit trails, transaction reports, and customer reports to meet regulatory and internal reporting obligations.
- **System Administrators** — manage portal users, define roles and workgroups, configure maker-checker workflows, and control system settings.

## Core Modules

Lango is organised into focused modules, each accessible from the main navigation sidebar:

| Module | What You Can Do |
|---|---|
| **Dashboard** | View real-time terminal telemetry and a transactions dashboard across your estate |
| **Customer Management** | Create and manage customers (merchants), their outlets, and assigned operators |
| **Inventory** | Track terminal stock, manage SIM cards, and view per-device details |
| **Terminal Management** | Define device configurations, manage estate groupings, and maintain terminal parameters |
| **Transactions** | Search, filter, and inspect individual transaction records |
| **Reports** | Export audit trails, customer reports, terminal status reports, and detailed transaction reports |
| **User Management** | Administer portal users, roles, and workgroups |
| **Master Data** | Maintain reference data — countries, bank regions, branches, MCC codes, card schemes, and more |
| **Settings** | Configure system behaviour, maker-checker rules, licence activation, and customer menu management |

## Key Concepts

Understanding these building blocks will help you navigate Lango confidently:

- **Customer** — A merchant or business entity that your bank has onboarded to accept card payments. Customers are the top-level record in Lango; all outlets and terminals belong to a customer.
- **Outlet** — A physical trading location belonging to a customer. A single customer can have multiple outlets, each with its own terminal profile and operator assignments.
- **Operator** — An individual who operates a terminal at an outlet. Operators are linked to terminals and their transaction activity is tracked separately.
- **Terminal** — A POS device deployed at a merchant outlet. Each terminal has a serial number, model, SIM card assignment, and a live status visible on the dashboard.
- **Device Configuration** — A named set of parameters (application bundles, terminal parameters, and estate groupings) that governs how a terminal behaves. Configurations are created once and applied to one or many terminals.
- **Maker-Checker Workflow** — A four-eyes control mechanism where one user initiates a change (the "maker") and a second authorised user approves or rejects it (the "checker"). Maker-checker can be enabled or disabled globally from Settings and applies to sensitive operations such as customer creation and terminal assignments.
